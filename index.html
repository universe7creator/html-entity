FAQ

What are HTML entities?

HTML entities are special codes used to represent characters that have special meaning in HTML, like < (&lt;) and > (&gt;), or characters not on a standard keyboard.

Why do I need to encode special characters?

Special characters like < and > are interpreted as HTML tags by browsers. To display them as text, you must use their entity equivalents (&lt; and &gt;).
